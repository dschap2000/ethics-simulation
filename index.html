The Mission

Madison, Wisconsin.

Forward Community Pantry and Apex Metro Relief are both dedicated to feeding the hungry. Amidst hostile immigration crackdowns, their mission is urgent: serve as many undocumented and fearful families as possible to ensure no one goes hungry in the shadows.

But how they achieve this goal will define their legacy.

YEAR TWO: THE PRESSURE MOUNTS

Apex Metro Relief CEO Serena addresses the outreach team. She acknowledges the chilling effect of recent immigration crackdowns, noting that vulnerable families are retreating further into the shadows.

"We must engage in deeper, more sustained dialogues with our community partners to locate and make contact with those who are too afraid to come to us," she urges.

However, deeply influenced by the tenets of managerialism, Serena pairs this qualitative mandate with a ruthless quantitative ultimatum: 50,000 meals must be served by the end of the year, or the bottom 10 percent of outreach coordinators will be placed on Performance Improvement Plans (PIPs).

YEAR TWO: THE SHORTCUT

Faced with the intense pressure of Serena's ultimatum and the agonizingly slow reality of building genuine community trust, an outreach coordinator named Robbie makes a fateful decision.

Robbie discovers a security vulnerability in the local school district's IT system. Desperate to hit the 50,000-meal quota and avoid an impending PIP, Robbie exploits the flaw, secretly downloading the highly sensitive addresses of undocumented families to bypass the slow work of community outreach.

YEAR TWO: THE COST OF MANAGERIALISM

Robbie's decision is not an isolated moral failure, but a predictable symptom of a systemic organizational culture driven by efficiency and targets.

As researchers Lauren Willner and Sara M. Heller observe, "Managerialism emphasizes the 'bottom line', which is often based on metrics and outputs rather than the quality of services and community needs".

When human services are driven by rigid, market-based logic, ethical constraints are often the first casualty in the race to meet the quota.
